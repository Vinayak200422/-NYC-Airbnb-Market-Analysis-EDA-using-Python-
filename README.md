Project Overview

This project analyzes the NYC Airbnb 2019 dataset (48,000+ listings) to understand pricing patterns, demand trends, availability, and key factors influencing rental prices across different boroughs of New York City.

The goal is to extract meaningful business insights that can help hosts optimize pricing strategies and assist travelers in choosing cost-effective locations.

📊 Problem Statements

Which neighborhoods have the highest number of listings?

How does price vary across boroughs and room types?

What factors influence Airbnb prices?

Which areas show the highest demand based on reviews?

How does minimum stay requirement affect booking trends?

📈 Key Insights

Manhattan has the highest average price (~$146/day), Bronx the lowest (~$77/day).

Brooklyn & Manhattan contribute ~80% of total reviews (highest demand).

Entire Home/Apt is the most preferred room type.

Most listings require short-term stays (1–3 nights).

🎯 Business Conclusion

Manhattan and Brooklyn show high demand but also high competition.
Outer boroughs offer lower pricing and potential opportunities for budget-focused hosting strategies.
